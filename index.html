Rudyard Kipling

The Nobel Prize for Literature winning author of The Jungle Book and Kim.

The famed novelist, short story writer and poet in 1895.

Here is a time line of Rudyard Kipling's life:

  • 1865 - Rudyard is born in Bombay, in the Bombay Presidency of British India

  • 1871 - Rudyard spends the first 6 years of his life in Bombay before being sent to the United Kingdom for his schooling as was the custom in British India. The next several years are miserable for Rudyard as he is bullied and neglected by his carer and her thirteen year old son at Lorne Lodge, a boarding home for children of British Nationals living abroad

  • 1877 - Rudyard’s mother, Alice returns from India and removes Rudyard from Lorne Lodge upon hearing of the ongoing poor treatment of her son. Rudyard spends the next few years finishing his schooling in the United Kingdom

  • 1882 - Rudyard returns to India as his parents can not afford to fund his entry to Oxford University, nor does he have the academic ability to get in on a scholarship. His father obtains a job for him in Lahore as an assistant editor of a local newspaper

  • 1886 - Rudyard’s first collection of verse, Departmental Ditties, is published

  • 1892 - In January, Rudyard married American Carrie Balestier in London at the age of 26. Rudyard and Carrie honeymooned in America and Japan before settling in Vermont. Their first child, Josephine was born on 29 December, the day before Rudyard’s birthday, and two days before Carrie’s

  • 1894 - The Jungle Book is first published. It remains popular to this day and has been adapted for film and other media

  • 1896 - Rudyard and his young family return to the United Kingdom where they settle in Torquay on the south-western coast of England. Kipling is now a famous man. In February Elsie Kipling, the couple’s second daughter is born

  • 1897 - The Kipling’s welcome their first son, John, in August

  • 1899 - On a voyage to the United States, Rudyard and eldest daughter Josephine both become gravely ill with pneumonia and Josephine later dies. Rudyard and Carrie are distraught and return to England where Rudyard throws himself into his writing to help cope with his grief

  • 1901 - The novel Kim is published. Notable for its detailed portrait of the people, culture, and varied religions of India, it tells the story of Kimball O’Hara and his adventures in the Himalayas

  • 1907 - Rudyard is awarded the Nobel Prize for Literature. He is the first English-language recipient and to this day, the youngest recipient to win the award, at the age of 41

  • 1915 - During the early years of WW1, Rudyard’s son John is accepted into the Irish Guards and is sent to the Western Front in France where he is killed in action

  • 1936 - Rudyard suffers a haemorrhage in his small intestine and dies less than a week later of a perforated duodenal ulcer

"Ship me somewheres east of Suez, where the best is like the worst,

Where there aren’t no Ten Commandments an’ a man can raise a thirst;

For the temple-bells are callin’, an’ it’s there that I would be —

By the old Moulmein Pagoda, looking lazy at the sea;

On the road to Mandalay,

Where the old Flotilla lay,

With our sick beneath the awnings when we went to Mandalay!

On the road to Mandalay,

Where the flyin’-fishes play,

An’ the dawn comes up like thunder outer China ‘crost the Bay!"

-- Rudyard Kipling, Mandalay published 1892
